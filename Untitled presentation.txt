Hydrometallurgical
Hydrometallurgical processing uses water-based chemicals to recover 90-99% of critical minerals like lithium, cobalt, and nickel (Beaudet et al., 2020)



Pyrometallurgical processing uses high-temperature smelting to extract metals, recovering fewer materials but handling a wider range of battery types (Beaudet et al., 2020)
Pyrometallurgical
By 2060 both methods run at near-100% efficiency, and every battery is processed at end-of-life through a government-backed industry.

Closed Loop System
Recovered materials feed straight back into new battery production, removing the need for new mining
The system follows a five‑stage cycle: Repair, Remanufacture, Resell, Repurpose, Recycle (Yamanouchi et al., 2026).
Only one‑quarter of global e‑waste was properly recycled in 2022 (IEA, 2024); by 2060, Canada meets its lithium, cobalt, and nickel needs through recovery alone.
